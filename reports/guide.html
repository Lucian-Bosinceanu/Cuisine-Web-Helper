1. Abstract

We are a team of students from "Faculty of Computer Science" Iași, IInd year, B1 group, and this is our Web Technologies project - Cuisine Web Helper. This article provides instructions on how to use this application, such as core features and admin & user functionalities.

2. Introduction

Cuisine Web Helper is a Web application that allows people to quickly find cooking recipes for their next delicious meal. Each recipe has multiple tags associated with it in regard to the ingredients used, cooking methods and tools, food restrictions, life style etc. The search engine is based on providing recipes that satisfies a list of tags provided by the user. In addition to this, the users have access to a series of articles that they can read in order to hone their cooking skills or learn something new.

3. Main functionalities

Cuisine Web Helper has two types of users: normal users and administrators. Both of them have a set of common functionalities such as searching of resources (recipes or articles), as described below. In addition, admins are responsible with content management. They can create, view, search, update and delete resources.

4.2. Login

This functionality is reserved only for admin users. From the main page, locate the login button from the upper right corner. Press it and go to login page. Insert your admin credentials, then click "Login". You will be redirected to Home Page where you will be able to see the last 15 recipes introduced in the system.

4.3. Search recipes

From the Recipes page, click on the search button from the upper left corner. A search sidebar will be visible, offering multiple options for recipe filtering, such as Search by title, Search by tags, Required Time, Difficulty or Food restrictions. Fill in the desired fields and click the Search button at the bottom of the search sidebar.

  1. Search by title: filter recipes by their title
  2. Search by tags: filter recipes by their associated tags. Only suggested tags are available.
  3. Required time: filter recipes by the time it takes to cook the meal. Select one or more options.
  4. Difficulty: filter recipes by the amount of skill required to prepare the meal. Select from Very easy to Very hard.
  5. Food restrictions: filter recipes by inserting associated tags. Only recipes that do not contain those tags will be displayed.

4.4. Search articles

From the Articles page, click on the search button from the upper left corner. A search sidebar will be visible. In the search field, insert a text and press the search button. All articles that have the inserted text in their name will be displayed on the page.
